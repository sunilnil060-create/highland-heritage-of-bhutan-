Environment Plan 01 · 5 Sessions

Highland Ecology & the Yak Herding Calendar

A multi-session lesson exploring the seasonal migration patterns of yak herding communities in Haa and Sephu — integrating ecology, geography, and oral history through film and field testimonies.

Environment Haa Sephu Grades 9–12 Film + Oral History
✦ LESSON OVERVIEW

About This Lesson

This multi-session lesson invites students to explore the seasonal migration patterns of yak herding communities in Haa and Sephu. Through documentary film footage, elder testimonies, and mapping activities, students build a rich understanding of highland ecology as it is lived and known by its communities.

Cross-curricular connections link environmental science with social studies, geography, and oral history methodology. The lesson emphasises the role of traditional ecological knowledge as a form of expertise, challenging students to think critically about how knowledge is produced and preserved.

✦ LEARNING OBJECTIVES

By the End of This Lesson, Students Will…

  • ✓
    Describe the seasonal movement patterns of yak herding communities in Haa and Sephu.
  • ✓
    Analyse documentary film footage as a primary source for understanding highland ecology.
  • ✓
    Evaluate how oral history recordings convey ecological knowledge across generations.
  • ✓
    Map seasonal herding routes and connect them to ecological zones and climate patterns.
  • ✓
    Discuss the relationship between cultural practice and environmental conservation.
✦ CURRICULUM LINKS

Cross-Curricular Connections

This lesson is designed for secondary students (Grades 9–12) with cross-curricular links to environmental science, social studies, and geography. It supports critical media literacy through the analysis of documentary film and recorded oral histories. Teachers may adapt it as a standalone unit or integrate it into a broader cultural studies module.
