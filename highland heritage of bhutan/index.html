A Celebration of High-Altitude Heritage
of Laya

The Royal Highland Festival in Laya is a vibrant celebration of Bhutan's high-altitude communities and their resilient way of life. By showcasing prized yaks and local craftsmanship, the festival honors the deep bond between the people and their environment, preserving a unique cultural identity for future generations.

✦ ABOUT THE PROJECT

A Digital Bridge
to the Highlands

Documenting the sacred rituals, oral histories, and environmental wisdom of Bhutan's high-altitude communities through an academic lens.

A Royal Thimphu College Initiative
01 / ORGANIZATION

Academic Stewardship

Led by RTC faculty and students in collaboration with local community leaders.

02 / OUR MISSION

Cultural Preservation

Creating a sustainable digital repository for future generations of Bhutanese scholars.

03 / THE VISION

Global Connection

Showcasing the resilience of highland life to the global digital community.

04 / PROJECT DEPTH

The Highland Narrative

This initiative integrates traditional ecological knowledge with digital archiving.

Our methodology involves year-long field studies across Haa and Sephu. We ensure that environmental wisdom is preserved for future research.
